What is Biomedical Engineering?

The application of engineering principles and design concepts to medicine and biology for healthcare purposes is known as biomedical engineering (BME) or medical engineering (e.g., diagnostic or therapeutic). BME is also traditionally used in the logical sciences to advance health care treatment, such as diagnosis, monitoring, and therapy. A biomedical engineer's responsibilities also include the management of current medical equipment in hospitals while adhering to relevant industry standards. This role, also known as a Biomedical Equipment Technician (BMET) or clinical engineering, entails procurement, routine testing, preventive maintenance, and making equipment recommendations.

Bio Printing

Bioprinting is an additive manufacturing process, similar to 3D printing, in which an object is printed layer by layer using a digital file as a blueprint. Bioprinters, unlike 3D printers, print with cells and biomaterials, creating organ-like structures that allow living cells to multiply. Despite being a relatively new technology, bioprinting has enormous potential to benefit industries such as regenerative and personalised medicine, drug discovery, and cosmetics.
